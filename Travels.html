Travels

Traveling has always been a passion of mine. I was privileged to experience a good bit when I was young, trips to visit my grandparents in Miami were frequent, a few times a year, and the idea that there were places other than where I lived with so many vast and minute differences was always fascinating to me. I drank in my NatGeo subscription with the incomparable thirst of a child in love with learning, I wanted to see every far flung jungle, every gorgeous exotic-creature-laden beach, to traipse across the beautiful mountainous expanses on my own expeditions, and see the great plains on safari! This was beaten out of me for a time by the hum-drum of everyday life and the weight of presumed responsibility to go about the process of growing up in a particular way; get a job, get a promotion, get a better job, get a house, get a wife, have a family, increase assets, put the kids through school, THEN finally be free to travel... Thankfully I realized this was nonsense and that I could always come back to everyday life at about 22, and promptly got rid of my apartment, rented a room from a guy off Craigslist, and put in my two weeks notice at work within the month, despite the rather large promotion they made sure to make me aware I was walking away from.
